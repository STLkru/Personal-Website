Abstract

 

  • Project: Android cell phone application that prevents drivers from texting and driving, but meanwhile enables phone functionality for passengers.

    •  

  • Operation Overview: The application is a process that runs continuously in the background of the phone. Using the accelerometer, when movement is detected at a rate greater than 15 MPH, a lock-screen display will take over the phone and concurrently disable all texting functionality and access to social media platforms. The display, however, will provide access to Google Maps, the user's Music library, and emergency contacts. Passengers will be able to unlock their phones via a bluetooth or an image processing routine. Bluetooth functionality is intended for those who do not have smartphones. With this feature, passengers must pair their device with the driver's device and designate him as the driver. If the pairing is successful, the lock screen display will disappear and texting functionality will be enabled. Using image recognition, and for those who have smartphones, the passenger must take take a picture of the driver's two hands on the steering wheel. The application processes the image and compares it to a series of stored images to determine whether to unlock the phone. Once the application detects movement at less than 15 MPH, the lock screen will disappear from the driver's phone and the routine will reset. For more information, check out the powerpoint below.
